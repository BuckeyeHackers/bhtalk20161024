Introduction

A hackathon is...

an event where people get together for an extended period of time and create awesome things, particularly with software or hardware.

A hackathon isn't...

an event for evil programmers to get together and break into systems and steal information.

Things to Know

The projects that win often aren't the most technically complex, but they're pretty, practical, and have a great UI/UX.

There will be recruiters checking out the awesome stuff you do and looking to hire students for internships or full-time positions.

You can put your experience and project from the hackathon on your resume to show employers that you love what you do and can accomplish great things!

Before the Hackathon

SLEEP!

Aim to get 8-10 hours of sleep each night two nights before the hackathon.

Have an idea!

Know what you want to build before going in.

Your idea may or may not change, but it helps a ton to have some sort of a direction, especially if you brainstorm how you could start making it before you begin.

DON'T start coding your idea until the hackathon formally begins!

Have a team!

A solid team either has members who can each do different parts of the project that require different skills, or who all have the same skillset if the idea only requires a few specific skills.

If you don't have a team, the hackathon coordinators will have some sort of matchmaker service for teamless participants to form teams.

Ready, Set, Hack!

Plan

Don't get into writing code until you know exactly what you want to do!

MVP

Think about the absolute minimal functionality of your idea and try to build that first.

Tools

High level languages and frameworks are your best friends.

Getting Tired

Sleep if you have to!

If you're too tired to get anything done, take a nap, or even go home and sleep for a few hours.

Winding Down

Finishing touches

Knock out any last minute features that might make a difference.

Try to make your app look pretty!

Relax.

Chill

A hackathon is a fun event, don't worry too much about the outcome.

Recognition and prizes are awesome, but it's okay if you don't win anything!

After the Hackathon

Reflect

What did you do right?

What mistakes did you make?

What would you have done differently?

Sleep!

You earned a break, now take one!

Takeaways

Make something awesome

Don't worry about all the little details

Have fun!
